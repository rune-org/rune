# Single Sign-On (SSO)

Single Sign-On lets your team sign in to Rune using your company's identity system. Users authenticate through your identity provider, and Rune trusts that authentication.

## Why Use SSO?

| Benefit                      | What It Means                                               |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **One login for everything** | Users sign in with their company credentials                |
| **Better security**          | Your identity provider enforces your security policies      |
| **Automatic access**         | New employees get access when added to your identity system |
| **Compliance**               | Meet enterprise security requirements                       |

## How It Works

When a user signs in via SSO:

1. User clicks **Sign in with SSO** on Rune
2. Rune redirects to your company's login page
3. User signs in with company credentials
4. Identity provider confirms the user is valid
5. Rune creates or updates the user account automatically
6. User gains access to Rune

## Setup Process

Setting up SSO involves these steps:

1. **Get information from Rune** - The Service Provider tab shows you what to give your identity provider
2. **Configure your identity provider** - Create a SAML application in your company's system
3. **Enter information in Rune** - The Identity Provider tab is where you enter your identity provider's details
4. **Test** - Verify SSO works before going live

## What You Need

To set up SSO:

- Admin access to Rune
- Admin access to your company's identity provider
- At least one password-based admin account (as backup)

## SAML 2.0

Rune uses SAML 2.0, the industry standard for enterprise SSO. This works with most enterprise identity providers.
